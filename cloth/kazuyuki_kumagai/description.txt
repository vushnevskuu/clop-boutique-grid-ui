A muted vest by Kazuyuki Kumagai, from the Japanese lineage of ATTACHMENT, where restraint is treated as a system: pared silhouettes, industrial calm, and materials pushed toward a lived-in neutrality rather than polish.

Cut with a deep V front and a narrow shoulder line, it holds close without being rigid. The front closes through a run of small dark buttons, with a slightly arched hem that breaks into a subtle point, keeping the lower edge sharp instead of rounded. Vertical seam work and discreet welted storage sit flat to the body, reading as structure more than decoration.

Inside, a pale lining dampens the surface texture and stabilizes the drape. The outer cloth appears linen-forward in a washed graphite tone, intentionally creased and uneven, letting light catch the grain and the stress points. A minimal neck chain and an internal hanging tape sit where a label would normally announce itself.

Worn as a mid-layer to compress a look into one continuous column: over a shirt when precision is needed, or directly against the body when the fabric’s friction and weight are the point. It functions as a quiet frame for the torso, designed to narrow the front and keep the sides controlled.

Fit: slim to regular, intended to sit close through the chest with a slightly elongated front.
